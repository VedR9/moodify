# User Interview Discussion Guide
## Spotify Music Discovery Research — Part 2

**Format:** 1:1 conversation, 20–25 minutes  
**Target respondent:** Spotify users who marked "Active Hunter" or "Actively seeks new music" in the survey AND/OR scored 4–5 on discovery importance  
**Goal:** Understand the emotional and behavioral root of the discovery problem — not just *what* fails, but *why* it matters and *what they do instead*

---

## Before the Call

- Read their Q18–Q20 survey answers beforehand
- Open Spotify on your own phone to reference features during the conversation
- Record with permission ("Mind if I record just for my notes? Won't be shared.")

---

## 0. Warm-up (2 min)

> "Thanks for making time. This is completely informal — no right or wrong answers, I'm just trying to understand how people actually experience music discovery. Tell me a bit about yourself and your relationship with music."

*Listen for:* how central music is to their identity, not just their usage frequency.

---

## 1. Their Current Reality (5 min)

> "Walk me through the last time you sat down to listen to music. What did you actually do — did you open a playlist, search for something, hit shuffle?"

*Probe if needed:*
- "Did you find what you were looking for?"
- "How often does that feel satisfying vs. just... settling?"
- "What does your home screen on Spotify look like right now?"

*What we're looking for:* Default behaviour vs. intentional discovery — most users reveal they default to familiar playlists even when they *want* something new.

---

## 2. The Stuck Moment (5 min)

> "You mentioned in the survey that you feel stuck listening to the same things. Tell me about a specific recent moment — what were you doing, what mood were you in, what did you actually want to hear?"

*Probe:*
- "What did you try? Did you search, did you go to Discover Weekly, did you ask a friend?"
- "What happened? Why didn't it work?"
- "What did you end up listening to instead?"

*What we're looking for:* The workaround. People who care about discovery always have one — TikTok, asking friends, YouTube rabbit holes, Reddit. That workaround IS the MVP opportunity.

---

## 3. The Discovery Gap (5 min)

> "You said you wished apps understood the *feeling* you're looking for, not just the genre. Can you tell me more about that — give me an example of a feeling or vibe you've never been able to describe to an app?"

*(Adapt this opening line to whatever they said in Q19 of the survey)*

*Probe:*
- "If you could talk to Spotify like a friend — what would you actually say?"
- "Have you ever tried typing a mood or a description into the search bar? What happened?"
- "When a song recommendation *does* land perfectly — what made it work?"

*What we're looking for:* The language they use. "Calm but not boring", "nostalgic but not sad", "focus but not clinical" — these are product requirements in disguise.

---

## 4. AI & Trust (4 min)

> "If Spotify told you *why* it was recommending a song — like 'this matches the tempo of what you played at 11pm last Tuesday' — would that make you more likely to try it?"

*Probe:*
- "What would actually make you trust a recommendation from an algorithm you've never met?"
- "Have you ever felt like Spotify *knows* you? Or does it mostly feel like it's guessing?"

*What we're looking for:* Validation of the "explainability" MVP direction. Survey shows R3 scored 5/5 on this — we want to understand *what kind* of explanation builds trust.

---

## 5. The One Thing (3 min)

> "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about how music discovery works — not just on Spotify, anywhere — what would it be?"

*Let them answer fully before any follow-up.*

> "And if that existed — would you pay for it, or expect it to be free?"

*What we're looking for:* Willingness to pay signal + confirmation of the core problem.

---

## 6. Close (1 min)

> "This has been really helpful. Last thing — is there anything about how you experience music discovery that you feel like most people don't talk about or apps don't understand?"

Thank them. Ask if they'd be okay being contacted for a follow-up if needed.

---

## After Each Interview — Note These Immediately

| Question | What to capture |
|---|---|
| What was their workaround? | TikTok / friends / YouTube / Reddit / other |
| What language did they use for "vibe"? | Exact words — these are product copy |
| Did they mention Discover Weekly? | Positively or negatively? |
| Free or paid tier? | Directly affects their pain intensity |
| Would they pay for better discovery? | Yes / No / Maybe with caveats |
| One standout quote | Something you'd put in a deck |

---

## Red Flags — Reschedule or Skip

- They primarily use YouTube Music or Apple Music (different discovery mechanics)
- They scored 1–2 on discovery importance in the survey
- They gave one-word answers to Q18–Q20 (low engagement, won't give rich interview data)
